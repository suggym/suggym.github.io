Initial Impressions and Interactivity

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

Initially I was mainly focused on the visuals, specifically the colourful 3D assets and their hoverable interactivity, playing short animations over each section to reveal each interactable asset.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

In my two minutes with the experience I hovered over all the landing page assets, clicked on the one marked 'Studio', clicked on 'Discover Studio', scrolled down to 'Meet Some of our Luni team members', tried to click/scroll on the images to learn more, clicked off the tab, accidentally side scrolled back to landing page, clicked on career, side scrolled back to landing page.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most amount of time engaging with the main navigation pages (Studio, Omada, Career, Life) rather than the scrolling through the actual content, purely due to the satisfying visual appeal of the 3D navigation 'spaces' rather than traditional tabs as the main 'attraction'.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action was switching through the main navigation pages using side scrolls and downward scrolls to explore the 3D art and the satisfying animation sequences between each one. As I am not a client or intended audience member of the site, much of the written pages were quite disinteresting to someone who has no need for that information.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of interacting with this site is being able to left side-scroll to the landing page. As the main navigation pages are referencing 3D modelling software, intuitively it feels as if you should be able to rotate the assets to view all it's sides and details, however you can only do this to a certain extent, and rotating too far ultimately will take you unintentionally to the landing page. Furthermore, if you accidentally, or try to scroll down or up you will also switch pages often unintentionally. Being able to accidentally switch pages is not standard in most web design, and proved to be very frustrating in the experience.

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of this experience was definitely the fun interactive elements hidden in each of the 3D navigation pages. Although serving no mechanical or navigational purpose to the site, being able to find little hidden interactions, such as being able to put the ball in the hoop/goal on the 'Omada' page, or flipping the egg in the pan on the 'career' page, is extremely satisfying. That element of gamification and fun is very rare in most traditional websites so it is satisfying figuring out that it is an option, and going through the pages trying to dig out and find every single element they've hidden.
