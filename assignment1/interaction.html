Initial Impressions and Interactivity

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

Initally I was mainly focused on the visuals, specifically the colourful 3D assets and their hoverable interactivity, playing short animations over each section to reveal each interactable asset.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

In my two minutes with the experience I hovered over all the landing page assets, clicked on the one marked 'Studio', clicked on 'Discover Studio', scrolled down to 'Meet Some of our Luni team members', tried to click/scroll on the images to learn more, clicked off the tab, accidentally side scrolled back to landing page, clicked on career, side scrolled back to landing page.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating part of interacting with this site is being able to left side-scroll to the landing page. As the main navigation pages are referencing 3D modelling software, intuitively it feels as if you should be able to rotate the assets to view all it's sides and details, however you can only do this to a certain extent, and rotating too far ultiamtely will take you unintentionally to the landing page. Furthermore, if you accidentally, or try to scroll down or up you will also switch pages often unintentionally. Being able to accidentally switch pages is not standard in most web design, and proved to be very frustrating in the experience.
