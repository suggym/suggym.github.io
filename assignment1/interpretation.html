Interpretation of Behaviours

1. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I believe the intended primary goal of this experience is to showcase their design team's creative style and technical proficiencies in web and app design to potential companies who may want to hire them, as well as showing off their very successful sports app ‘Omada’ and the team behind it.

2. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

By having mainly visuals, animation and creative interactive elements at the forefront of their site navigation, whilst having most of the worded information buried in sub-tabs, definitely shows a priority in showcasing their visual style and web development skills and creativity. They have put the ‘Omada’ page first to further express their studio’s successes by putting their successful app in front. The focus on interactivity and fun, through the user being able to play with the soccer and basketballs on the Omada page, and explore other interactive elements across the other pages, further communicates their primary goal to show their talents as a studio. The idea would be to pull a company's attention with the visuals first, and then allow that interest to take them to deeper parts of the page, exploring the various creative interactive elements along the way, to actually get the information they would need to hire or work for them. The website reads almost like a digital portfolio for the company, showing of their main app success, as well as their technical web development and 3D art skills.

3. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

At first impression I think this is definitely a short term maybe 10-20min interactive experience as it does not necessarily need to support multiple visits as a company website that does not sell products or services on the site. As much of their work from what I've gathered is based off-site, i.e. both of their mobile apps, and their careers page being separate, as well as the whole site functioning more like a portfolio piece, they are unlikely to update it as often as say a clothes shopping website would. Many of this sites visitors would be going to the site either to download one of their apps, find information on the studio's activities/style/branding for potential hire, or to find a career/see the career life at their studio, all of which take less than 20minutes and usually would not require multiple visits.

4. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

I think this site is designed to make a big and beautiful first impression to potential clients, customers, and future employees without the need for repeat or long site visits. Using main navigation pages that are 90% visuals and artwork communicates that they want to make an extremely good first impression for the user. The limited amount of navigation pages and minimalist style of writing, mainly using quotes and short sentences, also communicates that the site mainly offers short-term and non-repeat interactions through the site being holistically not that complex or large in content. No user activity is able to be saved, and most of the links on the site redirect the user off the site to their more specific user goals, e.g. the careers page redirecting to a different site dedicated to careers and the Studio and Omada pages each linking to their Luni and Omada app downloads. Redirecting users off the site communicates that this site operates as a way finding point with the Luni company at it’s crux, the different little ‘islands’ of navigation on the landing page further communicating that the company holds a lot of different aspects, and that the Luni site itself is not designed to be spent hours on or designed for repeat visits.

5. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

This website mainly references 3D modelling design and software media forms through the large 3D assets providing the main navigation for the site, user hover actions rotating and moving the elements like you would in 3D modelling software like Blender or Maya.

6. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These explicit 3D modelling references encourage users to assume much of the website is rotatable or interactive in a way that makes the elements and assets move or be animated like they would in a Blender viewport. On each of the main pages of Luni dragging your mouse up/down or side to side adjusts the 'viewport' and perspective of the main visual assets like how it would in a 3D modelling software.

7. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The 3D modelling references communicate that I should be focused on the visual and technical splendour of this 'Luniverse' they have created, the user made to feel as if they are in the world of an art piece, the floating assets almost like an otherworldly tiny 'Luni' universe. The pastel coloured and rounded 3D assets evokes feelings of calm and relaxation for the viewer, as to portray the company as relaxed and chill and furthermore the focus on interactivity and fun in the 3D models, through the user being able to play with the soccer and basketballs on the Omada page, and explore other interactive elements across the other pages, communicates to the user that they should be having fun when interacting with this experience, and therefore should have fun engaging with the studio's products .
