I spent the most amount of time engaging with the main navigation pages rather than the scrolling through the content, purely due to the satisfying visual appeal of the 3D navigation 'spaces' rather than traditional tabs.
Although serving no mechanical or navigational purpose to the site, being able to find little hidden interactions, such as being able to flip the egg in the pan on the Career page, is extremely satisfying.
They have put the ‘Omada’ page first to further express their studio's focus on interactivity and fun by putting their successful app in front.
The website reads almost like a digital portfolio for the company, showing of their main app success, as well as their technical web development and 3D art skills.
The idea would be to pull a company's attention with the visuals first, and then allow that interest to take them to deeper parts of the page, exploring the various creative interactive elements along the way, to actually get the information they would need to hire or work for them.
I believe the intended primary goal of this experience is to showcase their design team's creative style and technical proficiencies in web and app design.
Many of this sites visitors would be going to the site either to download one of their apps, find information on the studio’s activities/style/branding for potential hire, or to find a career/see the career life at their studio.
